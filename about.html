The Concept

I Hate Taste Slop is a three-page web statement about AI-generated content. Each page documents a different category of what critics call "slop": AI-produced work that fills space without filling meaning. Written slop. Visual slop. Audible slop.

The central irony is structural, not incidental: the philosophical thesis criticising all of this — the voice you see being typed across each page — is itself generated by an AI. The very thing it is criticising.

This is not a bug. It is the point.

The Loop

The thesis does not stop. When it reaches its end, it slides off the screen and begins again — the same words, retyped, from the beginning. This is also intentional.

AI-generated content does not end. It does not conclude. It produces, and produces, and produces — the same statistical patterns recurring under the surface of every new sentence, the same averaged gestures of thought cycling through every new paragraph. The loop is not a technical limitation. It is an accurate description of how this works.

You may notice, on your second or third read, that you are reading the same critique you already read. So is the machine. It does not know this. It has no memory of having said it before. This, too, is the point.

The Contradiction

We built a machine that can write. We gave it everything humans ever wrote — their grief, their arguments, their discoveries — and it learned the grammar of thought without, perhaps, ever having a thought. Then we asked it to produce content at scale. And it obliged.

This site asks the machine to look in the mirror. The result is not confession. It is performance. But performance, done honestly enough, starts to resemble something.
